Research

My research interest covers the following areas:

Chemical Engineering Process Dynamics and Control, Advanced Process Control, Process Monitoring and Optimisation, Fault Detection and Diagnosis, Fault Tolerant Control Systems, Intelligent Control Systems, Machine Learning.

Research Project

1. Research Fellowship in Pharmaceutical Process Characterisation and Data Analysis, University of Leeds. Worked on resolving issues of sticking in the manufacture of pharmaceutical tablet through Big Data Analysis using several machine learning algorithms. It was a collaboration between the University of Leeds and Pfizer Global Research Sandwich, and part of Advanced Digital Design of Pharmaceutical Therapeutics (ADDoPT) project involving select UK universities, SMEs and the UK pharmaceutical industry. 2019

2. Doctoral Research on Fault Tolerant Control Systems Design and Newcastle University where I conducted research on the state-of-the-art data-based fault monitoring techniques and fault tolerant control strategies for processes in the oil and gas industry, particularly for distillation processes. Successfully designed four FTCS approaches which were implemented through simulation on three distillation processes with varying complexities, including an interactive dynamic crude distillation unit (CDU) using MATLAB and HYSYS. Also investigated control system availability, reliability and performance in the presence of control system component faults. 2014 – 2018

3. Research/consultancy work for the Research & Knowledge Exchange Services of the University of Strathclyde where I worked on Multi Sensor Technology (Muse-Tech) Project, funded under the Seventh Framework Programme of the European Community for Research, Technological Development and Demonstration Activities to develop multi-block chemometric calibration models for multi-sensors suitable for use in the food processing industry. 2016
